📖 User Manual

A friendly walk-through of every feature in GenomeIQ. Click any sidebar item to jump there.

🚀

Quick start in 60 seconds

  1. Open the Analyze tab.
  2. Click any sample button (BRCA1 / INS / APP / ACTB) or paste your own DNA sequence.
  3. Press Analyze Sequence.
  4. Read the verdict, probabilities, saliency map and circular plot.
  5. Click PDF in the top-right to download a report.
  6. Switch to the Copilot tab and ask a follow-up question (e.g. "Why is this Cancer?").
🔬 1. Analyze — predict disease from a DNA sequence

What it does: Takes a DNA sequence and predicts whether it carries markers of Cancer, Diabetes, Alzheimer's, or whether it is Normal (housekeeping).

How to use:

  1. Paste a DNA sequence (only A, T, G, C, N characters) into the text box, or pick one of the sample buttons.
  2. Watch the live Length, GC%, and Valid indicators update as you type.
  3. Choose a Model backbone in the Analysis Options card:
    • TFIDF — fast k-mer ensemble (best accuracy on this dataset).
    • DNABERT-2 — fine-tuned 117M-parameter genomic transformer.
    • Ensemble — averages both models for the most robust answer.
  4. Toggle the optional analyses:
    • Saliency explanation — colors each base by how much it contributed to the prediction.
    • Out-of-distribution check — warns if your sequence looks unlike anything in training.
    • Similar sequence search — returns the 5 most similar known sequences from the training corpus.
  5. Click Analyze Sequence. Results appear on the right.

What you see in the results:

  • Verdict card — predicted class, color-coded confidence gauge, OOD risk banner.
  • Class probabilities — how the model split its confidence across the four classes.
  • Sequence composition — A/T/G/C/N base counts, GC%, entropy, complexity.
  • Circular sequence map — outer ring = GC profile, middle ring = saliency hotspots, inner rings = ORFs (forward/reverse strand).
  • Saliency map — base-by-base coloring (slate → amber → rose) showing what drove the prediction.
  • Closest known sequences — top similar training sequences with their disease labels (sanity check).
  • Open Reading Frames (ORFs) — protein-coding stretches in both strands and three frames.

Pro tips: If confidence is low, switch the backbone or try the Ensemble mode. If OOD risk is "high", treat the prediction as suggestive — your sequence may be from a region the model hasn't seen.

💬 2. Copilot — ask AI to explain anything

What it does: A retrieval-augmented chat assistant. It searches a curated knowledge base of disease and gene facts, then asks an LLM to answer your question grounded in those facts.

How to use:

  1. Pick a provider from the dropdown:
    • DEMO — template-based answers, always works offline, no key needed.
    • GEMINI — Google Gemini 2.5 Flash (free 1500 req/day, requires GEMINI_API_KEY in .env).
    • OLLAMA — fully local Llama via Ollama runtime (offline, private).
  2. Click any of the suggested questions, or type your own and press Enter.
  3. Read the answer. Each AI response shows the retrieved sources as small chips below it — these are the knowledge-base facts used to ground the answer.
  4. If you have already analyzed a sequence, the right rail shows an "Active prediction context" card. The Copilot will weave that prediction into its answer (e.g. "Why is my prediction Cancer?").

Good questions to try:

  • What is BRCA1 and how does it relate to cancer?
  • Explain the role of APP and PSEN1 in Alzheimer's disease.
  • Why is my Diabetes prediction low confidence?
  • How does GenomeIQ's classifier work?
  • What does the OOD detector mean by high-risk?

Note: The Copilot will refuse to give medical advice. It is a research/education aid only.

🧪 3. Mutation Lab — compare reference vs variant

What it does: Takes two DNA sequences (a reference and a variant), aligns them base-by-base, and reports every mismatch along with its predicted protein-level effect and how the disease prediction shifts.

How to use:

  1. Paste your reference sequence in the left box.
  2. Paste the variant sequence in the right box. (Or click Load demo pair to try it instantly.)
  3. Click Compare Sequences.

What the report shows:

  • Aligned length, total mismatches, and mutation rate.
  • Reference and variant predictions side-by-side.
  • Probability shift — bidirectional bars showing how each class probability changed.
  • Mutation events table — for each mismatch: position, ref→alt base, codon change, amino-acid change, and effect category (synonymous / missense / nonsense / stop-loss).

When to use: You suspect a single-nucleotide variant might change disease status. The classifier acts as a "second opinion" alongside the AA-level effect.

📦 4. Batch Upload — classify many sequences at once

What it does: Accepts a multi-record FASTA file and runs the classifier on every sequence.

How to use:

  1. Prepare a FASTA file (each record starts with >identifier on its own line, sequence on subsequent lines).
  2. Click the file picker. .fa, .fasta, and .txt are accepted.
  3. Wait for processing. Results appear in a sortable table.

Result columns: ID, length, predicted class (color-coded badge), confidence, and status (OK / error message).

📚 5. Knowledge Base — disease & gene reference

What it does: A static reference of the four disease classes and their key marker genes, linked to NCBI gene pages.

How to use: Just browse the cards. Each card shows a description, key genes, biological pathways and external references.

📄 6. PDF Report download

What it does: Generates a formatted PDF of your most recent analysis — verdict, OOD risk, probabilities, sequence statistics, similar hits, ORFs, top saliency regions and the input sequence.

How to use: After running an analysis, click the blue PDF button in the top-right header. The file downloads as genomeiq-report-<timestamp>.pdf.

🌙 7. Dark mode + theme toggle

The sun/moon button in the top-right toggles between light and dark themes. Your choice is saved in localStorage and respected across sessions. The Composition doughnut and the Circular plot re-render with theme-appropriate colors.

⚠️ 8. Limitations & disclaimer
  • Trained on a finite curated corpus (~500 unique parent sequences after dedup, expanded to 22k via sliding-window augmentation).
  • Four broad disease categories only. Real disease risk depends on far more than k-mer signatures.
  • Out-of-distribution sequences may produce confident-looking but unreliable predictions — heed the OOD banner.
  • The Copilot LLM may occasionally produce errors despite retrieval grounding. Always cross-check medical claims against the cited sources.
  • Not for clinical decision-making. Research preview only.

Problem
DNA → disease prediction is hard
  • Genomes are long, noisy, and high-dimensional.
  • Disease-related markers are buried inside thousands of bases.
  • Traditional bioinformatics tools (BLAST, alignment) require known references.
  • We need a tool that takes any DNA sequence and tells the user something useful.

GenomeIQ

DNA disease classifier with explainability, OOD detection and similarity search.

GenomeIQ analyses DNA sequences for disease-associated gene markers using a TF-IDF + ensemble (Random Forest + calibrated Linear SVM + Logistic Regression) classifier. The pipeline includes group-aware K-fold cross-validation, sliding-window data augmentation, calibrated probabilities and saliency-based interpretation.

  • Supported classes: Cancer, Diabetes, Alzheimer's, Normal
  • Saliency-style highlighting of contributing regions
  • OOD detector flags sequences outside the training distribution
  • BLAST-lite nearest-neighbour lookup against the training corpus
  • Mutation impact analyser (synonymous / missense / nonsense / stop loss)
  • FASTA batch prediction
  • Circular sequence map with ORF + GC + saliency overlays
  • Downloadable PDF analysis report
  • DNABERT-2 transformer backbone (frozen + fine-tuned variants)
  • RAG-powered Genomics Copilot with Gemini / Ollama / offline-demo providers
